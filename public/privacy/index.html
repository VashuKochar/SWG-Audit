Privacy policy

SWG Audit protects user privacy. This policy describes how SWG Audit collects, uses, discloses, and safeguards information when the site and services (the "Service") are used.

1. Information collected

a. Information provided

  • Business email: Verification to access simulations requires a business email address. Used only for access control and abuse prevention.
  • Captcha: SWG Audit uses Google reCAPTCHA. Google's privacy policy applies to reCAPTCHA data: Google Privacy Policy.
  • Session cookie: A signed session cookie records that verification succeeded. No personal data is stored in the cookie beyond a "verified" flag.
  • Uploaded files: The data exfiltration simulation processes and temporarily stores uploaded files for the simulation only.

b. Automatically collected information

Server logs may capture device, browser, IP address, and usage patterns. SWG Audit does not use third‑party analytics cookies at this time.

2. Use of information

  • Provide, operate, and maintain the Service.
  • Control access to simulations (verification gate).
  • Prevent abuse.

3. Sharing of information

SWG Audit does not sell or rent personal information. Information may be shared only if required by law or to protect the rights, property, or safety of SWG Audit, users, or others.

4. Data retention

Uploaded files and simulation data are stored only as long as necessary and are deleted automatically after a short period (e.g. 10 minutes). Session data is cookie‑based and expires after 24 hours. SWG Audit does not retain the business email after the session.

5. Security

SWG Audit implements reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect information. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.

6. Rights

Depending on jurisdiction, users may have rights to access, correct, or delete personal information. To exercise these rights, open an issue in the repository or contact the maintainers.

7. Contact

For questions about this privacy policy, open an issue in the repository.

All tests are non-malicious and safe for production environments. No real threats are delivered. By continuing, you agree to our Terms of Use and Privacy Policy.
